Calvin Coolidge and Theodore Roosevelt

Calvin Coolidge and Theodore Roosevelt were two US government presidents who have different views of the role of the Federal Government. As the world becomes more advanced, social problems increase, requiring policies to be established, so there are many disagreements. According to President Coolidge's speech, he argued that freedom organizers were people who fought to free themselves from the shackles of government policy. Coolidge supported the growing effort to raise society's moral standards, but he cautioned people about the need to understand the impact of the branches of government. In addition, he promoted the rights of individuals and understood the difficulties of citizens. He emphasized the importance of the Declaration of Independence as the inspiration for free government throughout the world. When the government actively listens to its citizens' concerns and translates them into effective laws that tackle real problems, it can spark a wave of hope and satisfaction. These laws are not just about individual happiness, but also give collective insight from government decisions. Coolodge also emphasized the importance of low taxes. He believed that it would lead to prosperity and subjugation for the Americans when everyone has enough funds to serve their own concentration. As a citizen, the government has a responsibility to ensure that every citizen is treated equally and raise their own voice. The benefits of individual rights bring greater opportunities for the personal, such as improving the standard of their lives.

In contrast, President Roosevelt believed that the government should have more power and be more active to oppose private interests, and promote the interests of the many over the special interests of the few. He was concerned about powerful businesses going beyond government control. He wanted to tighten government control over citizens to protect mutual benefit instead of reducing the government's impact on the people of Coolidge. For this purpose, Roosevelt represented the people using the influence he could bring as president. He used that status as a weapon to reduce the rights of private businesses. As a result, the government has the right to free access to the books of any corporations to prevent corporate misconduct. To understand the people, they put a high value on the states' rights. Through the states, they know whether those policies are suitable for them or not. Even so, Roosevelt was worried that people who do not understand politics will give opinions that will cause damage to the whole country. He emphasized the importance of escaping from the control of money in politics as he was concerned about the imbalance in the rights of people. For all that, he focuses attention on self-government, and the need to combine practice common sense. In other words, he acts for the common good of the people and community.

As a citizen, I hold up the protection and expansion of people's rights. Even though both presidents protected the rights of people, they have different political philosophies. On the other hand, I concur with Roosevelt's point of view about how he promoted the collective over the individual. In spite of this, President Coolidge's responsive and relevant policies will help to reduce the impact of the government on people and make them more comfortable with their decisions in life. In fact, this will increase inequality or disparities between different regions or groups by following natural laws. Meanwhile, self government allows decisions to be made by those who understand the specific needs and challenges of our communities. This can lead to more accessible and pertinent policies that are more suitable for peoples' needs, and also avoid being influenced by private forces. However, it will be difficult for people to raise their voice if the government does not have good intentions and the decisions they make are not suitable to save their own rights. Overall, both have their strengths and weaknesses, which is great to combine President Coolidge's understanding of citizen worries and the strict control of private businesses, the promotion of collegiality of Theodore Roosevelt's policies, and adjust their policies to suit each different situation.
